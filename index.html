Vocabulary Review Money, Work & the Gig Economy ⏱ 15 min ▼
🎯 These are the key words and expressions for this unit. Review them carefully — you'll need them in the Q&A, the conversation activity, and the reading that follows.
📋 Vocabulary by Category
💼 Work & Contracts
contract — written work agreement
freelance — self-employed, project-by-project
permanent — long-term, stable job
temporary — short-term job
gig — a single, short-term job or task
be laid off — lose your job (company's decision)
hiring — looking for new employees
💰 Money & Benefits
pension — retirement income
sick pay — paid time off when ill
vacation pay — paid annual leave
earn cash — make money
in exchange for — in return for
extra income — money beyond your salary
🔄 Key Expressions
sign up with — register with a company/service
work for yourself — be self-employed
make ends meet — have just enough money
figure out — understand / work out
no day is the same — varied, unpredictable work
🌐 The Gig Economy
gig economy — system of short-term work contracts
chore — routine household task
outsource — pay someone else to do a task
get someone to do sth — arrange for someone to do it
do it yourself — complete a task without help

Reading The Gig Economy — Three Texts ⏱ 15 min ▼
🎯 You'll practise reading for the main idea — a key skill that means understanding what a text is about without reading every single word. Then you'll think critically about the ideas.
📖 Read the Three Texts (5 min)

Read quickly. Don't look up every word — focus on the overall message of each text.

Text 1 — Job Ad

We're hiring in your area! Would you like to earn some extra cash? Are you happy to do freelance work from home? If the answer is "yes," then give Reviews4U a call. We're a software company that needs gamers to test our new range of PC games. We'll pay $40 for each gaming review you send us. No contract, no stress, and hours that work for you.

Text 2 — Article

Today's workers no longer expect to have the same job for life. The gig economy means that more and more people are working for themselves, offering their services in exchange for cash, but with no contract, no pension, and no vacation or sick pay. The way we take care of chores has changed at home, too — we get people to walk our dogs, clean our houses, and deliver our groceries. Interestingly, there are things we used to get others to do that we now do ourselves because of technology: we book our own flights and manage our own money online.

Text 3 — Business Profile

CeeCee Rogers, a tech worker in New York, was laid off in 2018. She knew it might take a long time to find another job in her field. "When I lost my job, it was a big shock. Luckily, I found out about GigGetters and I signed up with them. They have me do various gigs so I can make money while I'm looking for something more permanent. I enjoy it — no day is the same!"

➜ Did you notice the language in Text 2? "We get people to walk our dogs... we have our groceries delivered." This is a special grammar structure — causatives — and it's very common in English. Let's learn it properly.
Grammar Causatives — have, get & make ⏱ 20 min ▼
🎯 Causatives let you talk about arranging for someone else to do something — or forcing them to. They appear constantly in everyday English, especially when talking about services, work, and home life.

📐 The Rules

HAVE / GET + object + past participle — someone does a service for you
subject + have/get + object + past participle
I have my car serviced every year.  |  She got her nails done before the wedding.
HAVE + person + base form — you arrange for someone to do something
subject + have + person + base form
They have a cleaner come every Friday.  |  I had a mechanic check the engine.
GET + person + to + infinitive — you ask or persuade someone
subject + get + person + to + infinitive
She got her brother to help with the move.  |  Can you get someone to fix this?
MAKE + person + base form — you force or require someone
subject + make + person + base form
My boss makes us work late on Fridays.  |  She made her son tidy his room.
